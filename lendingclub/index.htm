Never lend money to these people!

by Alexander Trentin


It makes sense to look for the demographic group if someone asks you for money. Data from peer-to-peer lender LendingClub shows that the differences in probability of default or late payment can be substantial.

Never lend to people from the South

Loans of people in South Carolina have a double as high probability of getting into problems than loans of people in New England.



Never lend to people with low employment length

The lower people are employed in their current job, the higher the probability that their loans will run into problems.



Never lend to people with short credit history

People who got their first credit limit approved more recently have a higher probability not to pay back their loans.



Never lend to people who want to consolidate debt

Surprisingly, people who want to consolidate debt are more likely to default on their loans. The lowest risk are borrowers who want to pay back credit card debt.



Never lend to people to poor people

The higher the income, the lower is the probability that a loan will not be paid back.



Group people by demographic criteria

The differences are much higher, if all the demographic criteria are grouped to classify people according to their probability if they pack their loans.
